Best Time to Sail

The Croatian Adriatic offers excellent sailing conditions from April through October, with peak season June–September.

April – May
18–24°C
Mild temperatures, fewer crowds. Sea still cool at 16°C. Variable winds and occasional rain. Perfect for experienced sailors.
June
24–28°C
Excellent conditions. Reliable Maestral winds. Sea reaches 22°C. Manageable crowds. Best overall sailing month.
July – August
29–34°C
Peak season. Sea 25°C+. Consistent afternoon Maestral (15–20 kn). Busy marinas. Vibrant atmosphere.
September – October
22–27°C
Golden season. Thinning crowds. Sea remains warm (22–24°C). Growing Bura risk. Excellent value.

Route A: Kaštela to Vis & Back

185 nautical miles
7 days
Intermediate difficulty
Spectacular circuit through central Dalmatian islands — Brač, Hvar and Vis. Explore baroque towns with UNESCO heritage, vibrant nightlife and secluded coves. Pakleni otoci archipelago is unmissable. This route combines the most accessible and popular sailing destinations in Croatia with genuine cultural experiences.

Route B: Ploče to Lastovo & Back

200 nautical miles
7 days
Intermediate+ difficulty
Southern Dalmatian islands from Marco Polo's legendary birthplace in Korčula to the wild solitude of Lastovo Nature Park. This route combines cultural riches, isolated bays, UNESCO National Park and some of the least-visited sailing grounds in the entire Adriatic. More adventurous and less crowded than Route A.

Route C: Šibenik & the Kornati

150 nautical miles
7 days
All levels difficulty
UNESCO heritage, river sailing, national parks and one of the most dramatic seascapes on earth — the Kornati islands. 89 islands, islets and reefs rise from the sea like broken limestone teeth. Described by George Bernard Shaw as "the most beautiful archipelago in the world." This unique route combines river and sea sailing with spectacular natural scenery.

Sailing Tips

Essential knowledge for cruising the Croatian Adriatic safely and enjoyably.

📋
Documents & Permits
Carry ICC or national sailing cert, boat registration, and third-party insurance. Cruising permits (vignette) required at port entry. National Park fees apply to Kornati, Telašćica, Mljet and Krka.
⚓
Marina Booking
July and August marinas fill fast. Book ACI marinas well ahead online. Always call ahead for town quays. Anchoring is free outside restricted areas — carry 50m+ of chain.
⛽
Fuel & Provisioning
Diesel available in most larger marinas. Carry extra jerry cans for remote routes (Kornati, Lastovo). Supermarkets only in larger towns — provision thoroughly.
📻
VHF Radio
Monitor Channel 16 at all times. Croatian MRCC uses Ch 16 & 10. Weather broadcasts on Ch 67 at 0545, 1245, 2045 local. Essential safety kit.
🌤️
Weather Resources
Use Croatian Met Service (meteo.hr), Windy.com and PredictWind for routing. Bura can appear with little warning — always check forecasts before departure.
🚢
Chartering
Charter April–October, peaking July–August. Bareboat and skippered charters available from Split, Trogir, Šibenik, Dubrovnik, Ploče. Check inventory carefully.
